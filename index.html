Selected
Work.

four projects I'm still proud of — each a small window into what I was trying to understand at the time.
feat. i · mmxxiv · data science
— feature 01

Renewable Energy
Impact Analysis.

A data science study of 6,270+ energy records from Appleton Tower, evaluating carbon reduction strategies for the University. Half-hourly electricity, heat, and solar generation data were cleaned, aligned, and merged into a single analysis pipeline so that trends could be compared across demand, supply, and seasonality rather than in isolation.

The most interesting result was also the least flattering: despite a 26kW solar installation, the panels offset only 4.49% of peak monthly demand. That turned the project from a simple reporting exercise into something closer to decision support.

PythonPandasMatplotlibTime series
feat. ii · mmxxiv · backend
— feature 02

Geospatial
REST API Service.

A production-ready Spring Boot microservice for geospatial calculations, coordinate validation, and region-based queries. The service exposed endpoints for distance calculations, proximity checks, next-position prediction, and region inclusion, with health monitoring provided through Spring Actuator and a test suite built around TestRestTemplate, JUnit, and AssertJ.

What I enjoyed most was the balance between correctness and engineering discipline — handling malformed input cleanly, designing predictable responses, and writing enough integration tests to trust the service when deployed. The final result achieved 85%+ coverage.

JavaSpring BootJUnitMaven
feat. iii · mmxxiv · simulation
— feature 03

N-body Solar
System Simulation.

A physics simulation exploring the motion of interacting bodies under gravity, built around three numerical integration algorithms and O(n²) force calculations. The codebase used an object-oriented structure to manage bodies, state vectors, and acceleration histories, while Matplotlib animation with frame-skipping kept it responsive enough to inspect visually.

This project sat at the intersection of mathematics, software structure, and performance trade-offs — I had to think about numerical stability, not just syntax. A good example of the kind of work I enjoy most.

PythonNumPyOOPNumerical methods
feat. iv · mmxxv · systems design
— feature 04

Curbie: Autonomous
Bin Collection System.

A System Design Project centred on building an autonomous bin collection system in which a TurtleBot 3 could dock with low-cost passive bin bases, guide itself using ArUco markers, and support repeatable collection routes. Software, sensing, and mechanical design all depended on one another — the robot could only behave reliably if the physical base geometry was designed with the software constraints in mind.

My contribution focused on hardware and CAD. In Fusion 360 I iterated the passive bin base, docking slot, and elements of the robot cover, working around real fabrication limits: print volume, material cost, structural weakness across split parts. CAD as an engineering tool, not just a drawing tool.
