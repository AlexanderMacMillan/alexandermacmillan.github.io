a brief portrait, in three or four paragraphs, of the person and the work.

I'm a second-year computer science student at the University of Edinburgh, with a particular interest in the quiet, foundational parts of software — kernels, protocols, and the code that runs underneath everything else.

Recent work has taken me through Linux kernel modifications on v6.12 (custom syscalls, per-process memory ledgers), networking protocol implementations, and a System Design Project building an autonomous bin collection robot called Curbie.

Away from the terminal I'm an Officer Cadet with the City of Edinburgh OTC, a Gold Duke of Edinburgh award holder, and an RYA Competent Crew — the outdoor life matters to me as much as the indoor one.

selected

feat. i · mmxxiv · data science
— feature 01

Renewable energy
impact analysis.

A data science study of 6,270+ energy records from Appleton Tower, evaluating carbon reduction strategies for the University. Half-hourly electricity, heat, and solar generation data were cleaned and merged into a pipeline that surfaced an uncomfortable finding: solar panels offset only 4.49% of peak monthly demand despite 26kW capacity.
